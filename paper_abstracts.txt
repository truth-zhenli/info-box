PAPER 4: Closed-Loop Machine Learning in Materials Discovery
================================================================================
Authors: Y Kim, S Kim, H Park
Journal: Archives of Computational Methods in Engineering, 2026
DOI: 10.1007/s11831-026-10733-1
Source: Springer (HTML scrape via curl)
Word count: 209

Abstract:
Closed-loop materials discovery transforms how new materials are engineered by integrating machine learning (ML) models with iterative validation, shifting from passive screening to sequential decision-making under uncertainty. Unlike open-loop optimization, closed-loop workflows can incorporate phase-map information and uncertainty-aware feedback to steer sampling toward phase-informed candidate regions, while reducing—but not eliminating—the risk of premature convergence to local or representation-induced optima. This review explores the foundations and architectures of closed-loop discovery, highlighting how learning paradigms and material representations—particularly molecular versus crystalline encodings—dictate what can be learned, generated, and validated. We conceptualize this framework as a predict–validate–update cycle and discuss how uncertainty quantification and acquisition policies balance exploration and exploitation trade-offs. We survey major decision engines, including Bayesian optimization, reinforcement learning, and evolutionary search, alongside acceleration mechanisms like batch execution and multi-fidelity evaluation. Furthermore, we examine candidate generation and inverse design, emphasizing the co-design of generators, representations, and evaluators to help ensure that candidates remain physically meaningful. We also underscore constraint-aware discovery and autonomous implementations, highlighting end-to-end systems for composition exploration and synthesis-condition optimization. Finally, we address key limitations, notably the reality gap between thermodynamic proxies and experimental synthesizability, and outline emerging directions, including large language model (LLM)-based agents, physics-informed ML, and federated networks, that may improve robustness and generalization across platforms.

PAPER 6: AI-driven multiscale modeling of biodegradable Mg-Lu alloys
================================================================================
Authors: X Zhang, H B Wu, J Chen, H Wang, X Fu, X Yu, J Ye, J Rong
Journal: Physical Chemistry Chemical Physics (PCCP), 2026
DOI: 10.1039/d6cp00456c
Source: Semantic Scholar API
Word count: 212

Abstract:
Magnesium-lutetium (Mg-Lu) alloys exhibit significant potential as biodegradable bone implant materials. However, their low alloying content necessitates the use of excessively large simulation supercells, resulting in high computational costs. Moreover, conventional empirical interatomic potentials face substantial challenges, thereby limiting the efficiency and accuracy of materials design. To address these issues, within the AI for Science paradigm, we developed a deep neural network-based machine learning potential (DNN-MLP) model to systematically investigate the crystallographic characteristics, mechanical properties, energetic properties, and dynamic corrosion behavior of Mg-Lu alloys. The results indicate that the constructed DNN-MLP model achieves a root-mean-square error (RMSE) of 2.57 × 10-3 eV per atom for energy predictions and below 2.39 × 10-3 eV Å-1 for force predictions. The lattice constants predicted by the DNN-MLP model show excellent agreement with experimental data and density functional theory (DFT) calculations. Meanwhile, the DNN-MLP model accurately captures the mechanical properties of Mg-Lu alloys and outperforms traditional empirical potentials, such as the Gupta potential; furthermore, the strengthening mechanism of Mg-Lu alloys is elucidated based on stacking fault energy analysis. In addition, the DNN-MLP model successfully predicts the corrosion current density and corrosion potential of Mg-Lu alloys. Overall, the DNN-MLP model offers high accuracy and reliability, enabling efficient multiscale simulation and optimized design of biomedical bone implant materials.

PAPER 7: Thermal EOR Performance in Vuggy Carbonate Reservoir
================================================================================
Authors: B Meghdouri, H.A. Akram, H. Kolivand, R. Al-Lawatia
Journal: EAGE, 2026
DOI: 10.3997/2214-4609.2026652011
Source: OpenAlex API
Word count: 199

Abstract:
Summary Thermal enhanced oil recovery (EOR) in vuggy carbonate reservoirs is strongly influenced by reservoir heterogeneity, fluid properties, and heat transfer mechanisms. This study presents a physics-based thermal reservoir simulation workflow applied to a heavy-oil vuggy carbonate system, with emphasis on data acquisition and pilot design. The model explicitly represents vug-matrix interactions using a dual-porosity dual-permeability configuration and incorporates thermal fluid and rock properties. Accurate thermal PVT modeling, viscosity measurements, and temperature-dependent relative permeability were found to be critical for realistically capturing steam behavior, multiphase flow, and oil mobility under thermal conditions. Sensitivity analyses demonstrate that steam injection temperature strongly controls recovery performance by governing oil viscosity reduction, heat penetration, and vapor mobility. In highly heterogeneous vuggy systems, temperature-dependent flow properties significantly affect steam sweep efficiency and the risk of vapor channeling. Multiple pilot configurations were evaluated to understand the impact of steam temperature, injection strategy, and well placement on thermal performance. Pilot simulation results provided key insights into injectivity, heat propagation, and production response, supporting the selection of a technically robust pilot design. The study highlights the importance of data acquisition and pilot evaluation to reduce uncertainty and guide full-field thermal development planning in complex vuggy carbonate reservoirs.
